GenuSim

An innovative teaching device that simulates the structure of the knee to improve medical professionals' ability to diagnose ACL and Meniscus tear injuries.

New medical students are often unable to diagnose knee injuries due to a lack of exposure and experience to these injuries as well as a lack of training/practice for knee specific injuries, primarily ACL and Meniscus tears. It is crucial that medical students be able to practice and learn how to diagnose knee injuries before working on human patients.

To attack this problem, along with a team, I ideated, designed, and modeled a knee ligament tear simulator using Fusion 360.

Function: The basic function of ligaments in the human body is to provide tension on bones to restrict ROM. To recreate this function, we connected six elastic wires to individual harmonic drive motors each of which can alter the tension in the wires. A taut wire indicates a healthy ligament, while a slack wire would indicate a complete tear. Because there aren't only two scenarios when it comes to ligament tears, each motor can drive to multiple positions to simulate a gradient of tear severity.

Model: Aside from the motors mounted on top of the femur, everything in this model is modeled from scratch. Every bone you see began with a sphere. Through extensive free form modeling I was able to create relatively accurate bones. Each wire was first sketched as a spline to an offset plane , and was then swept using a circle as the profile.

Tremor Resistant Self-Stabilizing Cane

A therapeutic cane that resists tremors from those with muscle degenerative diseases to increase stability, safety, and independence of its user.

Seeing the progress made with products targeted at people with tremors, most notably the Liftware spoon, to my friends and I, creating our own product that worked to ease the lives of those suffering from severe tremors was the most compelling. This product was also created as a tribute to my friend's grandfather who had been to the hospital on multiple occasions because his tremors from Parkinson's caused him to alter the position of his cane and as a result fall. Realizing that his grandfather likely wasn't the only one who went through these perils, we realized that there was a good market for the product we wanted to create. After assessing our target group and the problem we wanted to combat, we came up with the idea to create a cane that resists the forces of tremors to prevent unnecessary falls.

Structure/Components:

  • 4-Pronged Wide Base
    • Increases stability and reduces chances of a fall
  • Secondary Handle
    • Aids in transitioning from standing to seated position or vice versa
    • Placed 180 degrees from primary handle and staggered in height
    • Folds updward to increase compactness
    • Adds elevated surface that can be useful in a public setting, increases independence of user
  • Ball and Socket Pivot
    • Allows for better movement while maintaining full contact with ground
    • Allows for cane to essentially move independent of the base while walking
  • Cylindrical Housing
    • Restricts radius of movement so stick does not pass the center of mass of the cane as a whole.
    • Provides housing for potential electrical components.

After multiple concept sketches and brainstorming, we combined the best features from each design to create our final product.

Redesign for Recylability

Took a Rubik's Cube, created a detailled 3D model, conducted a DFMA analysis, redesigned the product to improve its recyclability, and used a 2k factorial Design of Experiment to compare the redesigned product with the original.

As a culmination of my Design For Manufacturability class, I was tasked with choosing a product, performing a Design for Manufacture and Assembly (DFMA) analysis on the product, creating a CAD model,, assembly, and detailed engineering drawings of the product, performing a Manufacturing cost analysis on the product using aPriori, redesigning the product, and finally performing a Design of Experiment where I compared the redesigned product with the original.
