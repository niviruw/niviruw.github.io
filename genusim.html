GenuSim

An innovative teaching device that simulates the structure of the knee
to improve medical professionals' ability to diagnose ACL and Meniscus tear injuries.

Functionality/Model

New medical students are often unable to diagnose knee injuries due to a lack of exposure and experience
to these injuries as well as a lack of training/practice for knee specific injuries, primarily ACL and Meniscus tears. It is
crucial that medical students be able to practice and learn how to diagnose knee injuries before working on human patients.

Model
  • Aside from the motors mounted on top of the femur, everything in this model is modeled from scratch. Every bone you see began with a sphere (shown below). Through extensive surface modeling, I was able to model these spheres into relatively accurate bones.
  • Each wire was first sketched as a spline on an offset plane. It was then swept using a sketched circle as the profile.
Function
  • The basic function of ligaments in the human body is to provide tension on bones to restrict ROM
  • To recreate this basic function, we connected six elastic wires to individual harmonic drive motors each of which can alter the tension in the wires. A taut wire indicates a healthy ligament, while a slack wire would indicate a complete tear . Because there aren't only two scenarios when it comes to ligament tears, each motor can drive to multiple positions to simulate a gradient of tear severity.
  • Each color coded wire represents a major ligament in the knee
    • Red = Meniscus
    • Yellow = ACL
    • Green = MCL
    • Blue = LCL
    • Salmon = Patellar Tendon
    • Grey = PCL
